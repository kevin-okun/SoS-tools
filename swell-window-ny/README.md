# Swell Window Explorer — New York / New Jersey

Which swell directions can actually reach your break? This tool maps the **swell window**,
the arc of open ocean a break can see, for seven breaks from Manasquan to Montauk, computed
over the real seafloor of the New York Bight.

Live: **https://tools.scienceofsurfing.com/swell-window-ny/**

## Breaks

Manasquan and Belmar (New Jersey); Rockaway, Long Beach, Lido West, and Gilgo (Long Island
south shore); Ditch Plains (Montauk).

## What it shows

- **Swell window (first order).** Straight sight lines from each break, blocked by Long Island,
  the New Jersey coast, and Block Island.
- **Refraction.** Back-traced ray paths over the real shelf for 6/10/14/18 s. The rays bend over
  the shoreface sand ridges and the shelf and terminate at genuine deep water past the shelf break.

It is geometry, not a forecast. It shows which directions reach a break and how the seafloor turns
the swell, not how big it gets.

## Data and references

- **Seafloor:** Global Multi-Resolution Topography (GMRT), high-resolution synthesis
  (Ryan, W. B. F., et al. 2009, *Geochem. Geophys. Geosyst.* 10, Q03014). https://www.gmrt.org
  The model domain is extended to the shelf break so rays reach true deep water.
- **Refraction:** linear wave-ray tracing over the bathymetry.
- **Diffraction** (described, not drawn): Penney, W. G. & Price, A. T. (1952),
  *Phil. Trans. R. Soc. A* 244(882): 236. https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/10.1098/rsta.1952.0003
